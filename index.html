I just got my PhD from the Department of Mathematics at University of Wisconsin-Madison, where I was advised by Laurentiu Maxim. My research is in singularity theory, with an algebro-geometric bent — in particular, I study the local topology of holomorphic functions by examining relationships between their Milnor fibrations and the information about their critical loci encoded by their Jacobian ideals.

I've been very involved in the Graduate Peer Mentor program here, as well as various other projects aimed at making things easier for students in the department, and I'm always happy to talk — if you're a current or prospective grad student here and want to know more about singularity theory, topology, algebraic geometry, life at UW-Madison, or anything else, please don't hesitate to get in touch!

I'm currently on the job market — inquiries from potential employers are, of course, also very welcome!
